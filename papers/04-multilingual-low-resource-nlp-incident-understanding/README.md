# Paper 4: Multilingual Low-Resource Incident Understanding

## Working Title

**Multilingual Cyber Incident Understanding in Low-Resource African Contexts: A Natural Language Processing Approach to Informal Incident Reports**

## Core Idea

This paper focuses on the multilingual and low-resource NLP challenge within CyberXTalk: understanding incident reports written in Nigerian English, Pidgin, code-mixed expressions, typo-heavy messages, and local-language-influenced narratives.

## Research Gap

Users often report incidents in natural language that does not match formal technical terminology. Reports may include slang, mixed language, misspellings, emotional descriptions, and incomplete technical details.

This makes incident understanding difficult for ordinary rule-based or formal reporting systems.

## Contribution

- Low-resource NLP framing for cyber incident reporting.
- Handling of Nigerian English, Pidgin, code-mixed and informal incident narratives.
- Language normalization and intent/entity extraction strategy.
- Evaluation of multilingual incident understanding for pre-CERT intelligence.

## Possible Language / Narrative Patterns

- Nigerian English
- Pidgin English
- Yoruba-influenced English
- Hausa-influenced English
- Igbo-influenced English
- Code-mixed complaints
- WhatsApp-style typo-heavy reports
- Informal banking and social media incident narratives

## Possible Research Questions

1. How can multilingual and informal incident narratives be normalized for structured incident understanding?
2. What linguistic patterns appear in low-resource cyber incident reports?
3. How does multilingual preprocessing affect classification and triage performance?

## Notes

This paper should avoid overclaiming language coverage. Start from realistic Nigerian / African reporting patterns and expand carefully.
